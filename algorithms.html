Algorithm Comparison

Below, you will see two sorting algorithms visualized. The goal of this visualization is to explain the difference in time complexity between the algorithms. Bubble sort may be a more intuitive algorithm, but is much slower when used for big numbers. Even with an array of length 10, (falsely) assuming that every iteration takes exactly the same amount of time, the Quicksort algorithm nearly always wins.

Scroll down to play around with the array size and speed

Bubble Sort

This animation represents a bubble sort algorithm. It iterates over the array, comparing two items, and swaps them if the left-hand item is bigger than the right-hand item.

Time complexity is O(n2).

Quicksort

This animation represents a quicksort algorithm. It takes a pivot, puts all smaller items to its left and all bigger items to its right. Then it recursively continues this process for each left and right half.

Time complexity is O(n log n).
